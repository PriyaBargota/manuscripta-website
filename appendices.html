Developer Updates

Week 1

Project Briefing

This week, we were assigned the Manuscripta project for the Systems Engineering module, working with IBM (a technology company providing AI tools) and Qualcomm (a company that makes computer chips).

Over the next 2 terms, we'll be developing an educational system for classrooms. The general idea involves teachers using technology to create and share learning materials with students on special low-distraction screens.

We will cover every stage of the Software Development Life Cycle, from gathering requirements and designing user interfaces to testing and presenting the final application.

Over the past week, we got to know our teammates and received our initial project brief from Prof. Dean Mohamedally. We've scheduled meetings with our clients for next week to gather specific requirements and clarify exactly what we'll be building. In the meantime, our team has begun researching the technologies we might use.

First Team Meeting

After receiving the details of our project and team members, we set up our communication and collaboration tools:

  • WhatsApp for quick day-to-day communication
  • GitHub for code repository and version control
  • In-person meetings scheduled after our weekly labs while feedback from TAs is fresh
  • Zoom for remote meetings when needed

Team organisation went well, and everyone is on the same page when it comes to our working values: showing respect, being proactive with required work, and communicating clearly. We've also prepared several general questions to ask our clients during next week's meeting. So far, it looks like we will work well as a team.

Research Assignments & Proposed Roles

Although the exact scope and requirements of the project are still being clarified with our clients, we have identified clear areas that will definitely require research and experimentation regardless of the final direction. To prepare effectively and align with our strengths and interests, we've assigned each team member a research area and proposed role:

AI Integration Specialist
  • Research: Offline AI models (OpenVINO and Qualcomm Snapdragon), quantisation, and IBM Granite integration
  • Responsibilities: Client communication, coordinating AI model selection and integration, project management
  • Skills required: Understanding of AI/ML concepts, API integration, project management, client communication
Windows Application Developer
  • Research: Windows app development, Microsoft Store packaging, and teacher interface design
  • Responsibilities: Building the teacher-facing Windows application, user interface, content creation tools
  • Skills required: Windows development (C#/.NET or similar), UI/UX design, desktop application development
Android & E-Ink Developer
  • Research: Android development, E-Ink display programming, ExecuTorch framework for mobile AI
  • Responsibilities: Building the student tablet application, optimizing for E-Ink displays
  • Skills required: Java/Kotlin, Android SDK, mobile UI development, working with display hardware
Network & Systems Developer
  • Research: Local WiFi networking, device-to-device communication, handling 30+ simultaneous connections
  • Responsibilities: Network architecture, ensuring reliable content delivery across classroom devices
  • Skills required: Network programming, backend development, understanding of protocols (HTTP/WebSockets), system architecture

Each team member will present their research findings at our next meeting to inform our technical approach once we have clearer requirements from our clients.
